How MedRecall Works

The app does not actually know whether you truly remember a topic. Instead, it estimates your memory strength based on your past review history.

Every time you review a topic and rate yourself, the following calculations happen automatically behind the scenes:

FSRS-MR Calculation Metrics

Calculates stability and retrievability profiles using historical tracking metrics:

Stability (S) Interval in days to drop to 90% recall
Retrievability (R) Probability of recall on day t
Difficulty (D) Cognitive complexity metric (0.1 - 0.9)
Lapses (L) Count of total retrieval failures

Overdue Bonus & Desirable Difficulty

Recalling a concept when it is heavily overdue provides an "Overdue Bonus" retrieval spike, significantly stretching out the next scheduled interval.

Smoothed Sigmoid Compression

Every subject has a target test date. Rather than forcing a sudden drop, MedRecall v2 uses a smoothed sigmoid curve to adjust intervals dynamically based on her relative daily capacity.

Instruction Manual

MedRecall utilizes adaptive cognitive storage stability rules to organize your study pipeline without forcing you to write notes or create cards.

1

Add Topics: Use the + button to log medical topics right after study sessions.

2

Test Daily: Review topics scheduled in the Today's Schedule queue. Recalling cards at their point of decay trains memory depth.

3

Score Memory: Use Struggled, Hesitant, or Mastered. The FSRS-MR scheduler dynamically reschedules further reviews.

4

Set Exam Dates: Map exam target days in your Syllabus panel. Important: Update the date of exam immediately after adding a subject to calibrate the adaptive exam compression scheduler correctly!
